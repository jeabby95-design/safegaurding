Safeguarding

Everyone has a role to play in safeguarding.

You can make a difference by staying informed, being vigilant, and reporting concerns promptly.


Key Information

What is Safeguarding?

Safeguarding means taking action to protect people from abuse, neglect, harm, or exploitation. It is about creating safe environments where individuals—especially children and vulnerable adults—feel supported, listened to, and protected. Safeguarding focuses on:

  • harm before it happens.
  • Recognising concerns early.
  • Responding appropriately when concerns arise.

    Signs to Watch For.

    Safeguarding concerns can show in different ways. Be alert to:

  • Unexplained injuries or repeated accidents
  • Changes in behaviour, mood, or confidence
  • Withdrawal from friends, family, or activities
  • Signs of fear, anxiety, or distress around certain people
  • Poor hygiene, lack of care, or unmet basic needs
  • Changes in communication or reluctance to speak
  • Not all signs mean abuse is happening, but they should never be ignored.

    Action Steps

    If you have a safeguarding concern, follow these steps:

  • Call 999 if an emergency exists.
  • Identify the Risk: Understand what the hazard is.
  • Report it: Tell the right person or authority.
  • Follow Up: Escalate if the response is inadequate.
  • Document: Keep records of your reports and actions taken.
  • Do not promise confidentiality.
