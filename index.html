Safeguarding

Everyone has a role to play in safeguarding.

Key Information

What is Safeguarding?

Safeguarding is the process of protecting children and vulnerable adults from harm, abuse, and neglect.

More Info
Signs to Watch For.

Changes in behavior, mood, or appearance. Other signs may include withdrawal, fear, or unexplained injuries. Not all signs are visible so look out for emotional and social changes.

More Info
Action Steps

If you suspect someone is being harmed or neglected, report it immediately to the appropriate authorities or your designated safeguarding lead.

More Info

Key Actions

  • Call 999 if an emergency exists.
  • Identify the Risk: Understand what the hazard is.
  • Report it: Tell the right person or authority.
  • Follow Up: Escalate if the response is inadequate.
  • Document: Keep records of your reports and actions taken.
