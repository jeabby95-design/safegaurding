Key Information

What is Safeguarding?

Safeguarding means taking action to protect people from abuse, neglect, harm, or exploitation. It is about creating safe environments where individuals—especially children and vulnerable adults—feel supported, listened to, and protected. Safeguarding focuses on:

  • Preventing harm before it happens.
  • Recognising concerns early.
  • Responding appropriately when concerns arise.

Signs to Watch For

Recognizing the signs of abuse or neglect is crucial for effective safeguarding. Some common indicators include:

  • Unexplained injuries or frequent bruises
  • Withdrawal from social interactions
  • Changes in behaviour, mood, or confidence
  • Fear, anxiety, or distress around certain people
  • Poor hygiene or unmet basic needs
  • Reluctance to speak or changes in communication
  • Not all signs are obvious and not all signs mean abuse is happening, but they should never be ignored.

Action Steps

If you suspect someone is at risk, take the following steps:

  • Report concerns to the appropriate authorities.
  • Document observations or conversations.
  • Offer support while ensuring safety.
  • Follow up on actions taken.
  • Do not promise confidentiality.
  • Call 999 in an emergency.
